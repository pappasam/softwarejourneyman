Preamble

Come inside, traveler. Put your membrane keyboard out to pasture, meditate upon your bloated operating system, and rest your weary fingertips. When using community-driven software, you are always among friends.

Why this blog?

Community-driven software is both flexible and diverse in its offerings. These traits cause novice programmers to struggle with tool selection, comprehension, and usage.

Core questions

  1. Given the sheer number of options, is there a "best" tool?
  2. When a suitable tool is found, what are its quirks?
  3. Once the best tool's quirks are ironed, how do I handle my team's quirks?

This blog exists mostly to answer the first question. Since my target audience is the early journeyman, I will treat my subject matter as "black and white" as possible. There are nuances to my tool selection, but discussing them here may confuse the reader. I will have tried many tools before settling on ones in the list, and will avoid writing about tools I do not understand. The second topic exists so others can avoid bugs I spend hours finding. The third gives me the opportunity to write about working with people. This may not see too much action, but it's there in case I get the urge.
