Software Journeyman

Journeyman

an individual who has completed an apprenticeship and is fully educated in a trade or craft, but not yet a master. Wikipedia

Come inside, traveler. Sheath your membrane keyboard, meditate upon your bloated operating system, and rest your weary fingertips. When using community-driven software, you are always among friends.

Why this blog?

Community-driven software is both flexible and diverse in its offerings. These traits cause novice programmers to struggle with tool selection, comprehension, and usage.

Core questions

  1. Given the sheer number of options, is there a "right" tool?
  2. When a "holy grail" tool is found, what are its quirks?
  3. Once the tool's quirks are ironed, how do I handle my team's quirks?

This blog exists mostly to answer the first question. Since my target audience is the early journeyman, I will treat my subject matter as "black and white" as possible. There are nuances to my tool selection, but discussing those them here may confuse the reader. I have tried many tools before settling on one in the list, and will avoid writing about tools I do not understand. The second topic exists so others can avoid bugs I spend hours finding. The third gives me the opportunity to write about working with people. This may not see too much action, but it's there in case I get the urge.
